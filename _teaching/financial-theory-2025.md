## Syllabus

Detailed weekly schedule and grading policy are shared on Campus Virtual.

## First Part

### Efficient Information and Predictability of Prices

* Fama (2013), "Two Pillars of Asset Pricing", Nobel Prize Lecture.
* Bailey (2005), Chapters 1 and 3.
* Schwert (2003), [Anomalies and market efficiency](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1574010203010240), Handbook of Economics and Finance, Vol. 1, Part B, Ch. 15.
* Harvey and Liu (2019), [A Census on the Factor Zoo](https://dx.doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.3341728).
* Feng, Giglio, and Xiu (2020), [Taming the Factor Zoo: A Test of New Factors](https://doi.org/10.1111/jofi.12883), Journal of Finance, 75(3), 1327-1370.
* Phillips, Shi, and Yu (2015), [Testing for Multiple Bubbles: Limit Theory of Real-Time Detectors](https://doi.org/10.1111/iere.12131), International Economic Review, 56, 1079-1134.

### Measures of Financial Risk and Risk-Return Relationship

* Tsay (2010), Chapter 1.
* McNeil, Frey, and Embrechts (2005), Chapters 1-2.
* Cochrane (2005), Chapters 1 and 4.
* Campbell (2000), [Asset Pricing at the Millennium](https://doi.org/10.1111/0022-1082.00260), Journal of Finance, 55, 1515-1567.
* Cochrane (2011), [Presidential Address: Discount Rates](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-6261.2011.01671.x), Journal of Finance, 66, 1047-1108.

### Stylized Facts of Financial Time Series

* McNeil, Frey, and Embrechts (2005), Section 4.1.
* Cont (2001), [Empirical properties of asset returns: stylized facts and statistical issues](https://doi.org/10.1080/713665670), Quantitative Finance, 1(2), 223.

## Second Part

### Portfolio Theory

* Berk and DeMarzo (2014), Corporate Finance (3rd ed.), Chapters 10-11.
* Bailey (2005), Chapters 5, 6, and 9.
* Cochrane (2005), Chapters 5 and 9.

### Capital Structure Theory

* Eckbo (2009), Vol. 2, Chapters 10-13.
* Berk and DeMarzo (2014), Corporate Finance (3rd ed.), Chapters 12, 14, 15, and 16.

## Third Part

### Option Theory

* Berk and DeMarzo (2014), Corporate Finance (3rd ed.), Chapters 20 and 21.
* Bailey (2005), Chapters 18-20.
* Hull (2023), Options, Futures, and Other Derivatives (11th ed.), Chapters 10, 11, and 15.

### Climate Finance

* Giglio, Kelly, and Stroebel (2021), [Climate Finance](https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-financial-102620-103311), Annual Review of Financial Economics, 13, 15-36.
* Hong, Li, and Xu (2019), [Climate risks and Market Efficiency](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jeconom.2018.09.015), Journal of Econometrics, 208(1), 265-281.
* Bolton and Kacperczyk (2020), [Do Investors Care about Carbon Risk](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jfineco.2021.05.008), NBER Working Paper 26968.
* Engle et al. (2020), [Hedging Climate Change News](https://doi.org/10.1093/rfs/hhz072), Review of Financial Studies, 33(3), 1184-1216.
* Stroebel and Wurgler (2021), [What Do You Think About Climate Finance?](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jfineco.2021.08.004), Journal of Financial Economics, 142(2), 487-498.
* Giglio et al. (2025), [Four facts about ESG beliefs and investor portfolios](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-financial-economics), Journal of Financial Economics, [164](https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-financial-economics/vol/164/suppl/C), 103984.

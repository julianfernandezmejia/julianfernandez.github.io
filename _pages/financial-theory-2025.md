## Syllabus

### First Part: Efficient Information and Predictability of Prices

**Readings:**

* Fama, E.F. (2013). "Two Pillars of Asset Pricing" (Nobel Prize Lecture)
* Bailey (2005), Chapters 1 and 3
* Schwert, G.W. (2003). "Anomalies and market efficiency," *Handbook of Economics and Finance*, 1(B), Chapter 15
* Harvey, C.R. & Liu, Y. (2019). "A Census on the Factor Zoo," [SSRN](https://dx.doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.3341728)
* Feng, G., Giglio, S., & Xiu, D. (2020). "Taming the Factor Zoo: A Test of New Factors," *The Journal of Finance*, 75(3), 1327-1370
* Phillips, P.C.B., Shi, S., & Yu, J. (2015). "Testing for Multiple Bubbles: Limit Theory of Real-Time Detectors," *International Economic Review*, 56, 1079-1134

### Second Part: Measures of Financial Risk and Risk-Return Relationship

**Readings:**

* Tsay (2010), Chapter 1
* McNeil, Frey, & Embrechts (2005), Chapters 1-2
* Cochrane (2005), Chapters 1 and 4
* Campbell (2000). "Asset Pricing at the Millennium," *The Journal of Finance*, 55, 1515-1567
* Cochrane (2011). "Presidential Address: Discount Rates," *The Journal of Finance*, 66(4), 1047-1108
